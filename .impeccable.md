## Design Context

### Users
Sejong University students and presenters rehearsing a talk shortly before class, demo day, or a team presentation. They need fast feedback on pace, silence, clarity, and script adherence without setting up a formal recording workflow.

### Brand Personality
Calm, precise, and confidence-building. The product should feel like a refined presentation command center: focused enough for practice, warm enough to lower speaking anxiety, and polished enough for a hackathon demo.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a Claude-inspired warm editorial system: tinted cream canvas, restrained coral CTA, serif display headlines, thin hairline borders, and one dark product surface that shows actual rehearsal feedback rather than decorative analytics. Synais remains useful for the dashboard/product-preview composition, but the final interface should feel more literary, calm, and hand-composed than generic SaaS.

### Design Principles
- Lead with the live rehearsal workspace, not marketing copy.
- Make metrics feel calm and interpretable rather than alarming.
- Use warm editorial typography and sparse coral moments to soften analytical feedback.
- Keep controls compact, direct, and demo-friendly.
- Preserve strong contrast, readable Korean text, and responsive layouts.
